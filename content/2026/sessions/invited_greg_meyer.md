## Bio

Greg Kahanamoku-Meyer is a postdoctoral researcher at MIT, working at the intersection of quantum computing, cryptography, and high-performance computing. He received his PhD from UC Berkeley in 2023. His research spans optimizing quantum algorithms for factoring, developing and analyzing protocols for demonstrating quantum advantage, and building high-performance tools for the numerical study of quantum many-body systems (including the GPU- and MPI-accelerated library *dynamite*).
